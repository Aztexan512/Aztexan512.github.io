Revenue Expansion

Multiproduct Household Cross-Sell Propensity & Revenue Expansion

This project is exactly what I look for when evaluating senior analysts who can move the needle on revenue — not just report on it. Luciano built a full propensity model to identify which single-product AutoShield Insurance households are most likely to convert to a second product within a 12-month window. Using Gradient Boosting on 150,000 synthetic households across a 3-year observation period, he isolated property quote abandonment and proactive outreach frequency as the two dominant behavioral signals — the kind of insight that goes straight into a sales strategy deck. The top two deciles alone capture 36% of all conversions, and the projected unconverted CLTV opportunity exceeds $280M. If you want someone who can connect predictive modeling to commercial outcomes and explain it to a VP without flinching, this is the work that tells you they can do it.

VOC Analytics

NovaStar Telecom — VOC Survey Analysis

Customer experience analytics is a crowded space, and most candidates can run a survey report. What separates a strong mid-to-senior analyst is the ability to build a predictive recovery system on top of that data — and that's what this project demonstrates. Luciano executed a full VOC workflow on 120,000 synthetic post-interaction survey responses, then layered in a Gradient Boosting Detractor risk model (AUC 0.81) to identify which dissatisfied customers should be prioritized for outreach before they churn or post publicly. Driver analysis surfaces resolution ease, wait time, and first-contact resolution as the top NPS levers — findings that map directly to operational decisions a CX or service operations leader can act on. The Streamlit revenue impact simulator shows he understands his audience: stakeholders need to see the dollar case, not just the model card.

Churn & Retention

Churn modeling is table stakes for a senior data analyst role — but this project shows the level of rigor and cross-functional thinking that separates a high performer from someone who just runs models. Built on a 225,000-row synthetic dataset, the XGBoost model (AUC 0.84) comes with full SHAP explainability so any business stakeholder can trace exactly which behavioral signals are driving attrition. The $32.45M CLTV-at-risk simulation does the work of translating model outputs into a business case, and the live Streamlit dashboard means the analysis doesn't die in a notebook — it gets used. What stands out here is the intentional framework extension to auto insurance non-renewal, which signals a candidate who thinks about transferability and industry applicability, not just in-scope deliverables.
